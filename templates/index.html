The Future of Books = the Web

No one owns the idea of a book, but somehow (historical reasons) the word ebook is almost synonymous with the Kindle.

FuturePress aims to free content from the prisons of current proprietary formats through building awesome open source technology and utilizing powerful web standards. All our code is licensed under the incredibly permissive Free BSD license.

The future of eBooks is not in the production of digital photocopies you can read by the pool. The future of eBooks lies in re-imagining the book as an open, easily accessible, immersive experience; a connected community of discovery.

Scott Abel The Content Wrangler

Read on any deviceAnywhere

Any device that supports a modern browser will work. It doesn't matter if it's an iOS, OSX, Android, Linux, Windows 8, etc. For offline reading it is easy to enable download of the Epub file or using the browsers local storage for those times in the subway.

PowerfulDocuments

Imagined an easily-authored web-based document that could easily be turned into a website just by being hosted! Well, you don't have to imagine. It's called Epub. The goal of Epub.js is to give developers an important tool they need to work with this wonderful packaged document, and to someday hopefully lead to native browser support for Epub.

DecentralizedDistribution

By publishing with the open standards Epub and OPDS, authors can take part in decentralized distribution where a book listing on their own server can support an unlimited number of listings in other locations or with larger distributors. For example, a book review on a personal blog might embed the author’s book into the review, allowing the review reader to sample different sections of the text, as well as acting as an additional distribution point. In another example, ebook retailers, such as Feedbooks, can act as aggregators of content while the book listing itself is sourced from the author’s own server.

Ebooks belong on the web. This technology has the potential to give more power and flexibility to authors, developers, and publishers. No longer will we be chained up at the mercy of Apple and Amazon.

Marguerite Avery MIT Press
